Refuge & Lay Vows Ceremony
with Geshe Sherab

March 14
4:30 – 6:00 p.m.

What is Refuge?

Refuge in Buddhism is a ceremony, a commitment, and a statement of faith. The core element is the practitioner’s commitment to seeking liberation from suffering. The triple gem in which one takes refuge is the Buddha, the Dharma (the teachings/path), and the Sangha.

What are the Lay Vows?

Ethical vows guide a practitioner in examining cause and effect (karma), as well as how the renunciation, patience, and honesty required of this practice illuminate the teachings.

You may take all five of the lay vows, none of the lay vows, or a combination of the five lay vows.

The five lay vows are:

  1. Abstain from killing living beings;
  2. Abstain from taking that which is not given;
  3. Abstain from sexual misconduct;
  4. Abstain from telling lies;
  5. Abstain from alcohol/intoxicants.
What does taking refuge with Geshe Sherab mean?

When a teacher gives refuge or an oral transmission or precepts, and so on, to a student, automatically a sutra-level teacher-disciple relationship is established between that teacher and the students receiving the transmission, precepts, etc.

This means that Geshe Sherab will become one of your teachers. Geshe Sherab does not have to be your “Root Guru” or “Main Teacher”, but by taking Refuge or Lay Vows with Geshe-la, you are agreeing to taking on Geshe Sherab as a teacher and that you are a student of Geshe-la’s.

What do I need to do to prepare? What should I expect?

Think about your own motivation for taking Refuge. Review the Five Lay Vows and think about them in context of your life and your spiritual journey. Reflect on each Lay Vow and decide if it is at the right time in your practice to take on such a commitment.

Do some pre-reading to help generate a strong motivation for taking Refuge and/or Lay Vows. This article is compiled of different teachings from Lama Zopa Rinpoche about Refuge and Lay Vows. This is a list of different articles and teachings on Refuge and Lay Vows by Lama Zopa Rinpoche and Lama Yeshe.

On the day of the Refuge and Lay Vow Ceremony, shower and wear nice, clean clothes. Brush your teeth before the Ceremony. Dress yourself with humility and respect for the Ceremony you are going to participate in. Generate strong Bodhichitta motivation. And rejoice in your merits!

At the end of the Refuge and Lay Vow Ceremony, Geshe Sherab will call each new student who has taken refuge for the first time to come up to the front of the Gompa. He will present you with your Refuge name and you will take a picture with Geshe Sherab.

This will be the opportunity to give Geshe Sherab a khata (white offering scarf) and an offering. This is not necessary, but a gesture of thanks and generosity. You can buy a khata in our Gift Shop or borrow one from our loaner basket. Please have your offering already prepared in an envelope to offer to Geshe Sherab. It is also nice, but not necessary to give an offering to the Center for organizing the Refuge and Lay Vow Ceremony.

Note: Any amount of offering given to the Three Jewels (Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha) with a pure heart will be magnified, so please do not feel pressured to give more than you can.

What do I need to do if I want to take Refuge and/or Lay Vows with Geshe Sherab?

We will be handing out Refuge Booklets, so please register by Friday, March 13 at noon so we can prepare.

Can I attend the Refuge and Lay Vow Ceremony even if I am not taking them or have previously taken them?

Yes! Please arrive by 4:15 p.m. as we will start the Ceremony promptly at 4:30 p.m.
