Festival Day
with Special Performance by Nawang Khechog and an Interfaith Service

July 6

1:00 - 5:00 p.m.

Enjoy a delightful afternoon of spectacular entertainment, a moving interfaith service, and refreshing snacks in a beautiful, relaxing environment. Festival Day is also His Holiness the Dalai Lama's birthday and World Compassion Day. We will share cake to celebrate his birthday.

Special Guest! Nawang Khechog will be teaching the class from his new book, “Awakening Kindness”.

Nawang Khechog is the first Tibetan Grammy Nominee and the most renowned Tibetan flautist in the world. A monk for eleven years (including four years as a hermit meditator in the Himalayan foothills personally guided by His Holiness the Dalai Lama), Nawang studied Buddhist philosophy and meditation. He now fills a unique spot in Buddhist culture as a teacher, performer, practitioner, and Tibetan freedom fighter and has won multiple awards from Tibet, India, Nepal and the US. Nawang has collaborated with many prominent artists including Phillip Glass, Patti Smith, and the Beastie Boys. Through his love of music. Nawang brings the beauty of his and other countries' ancient ritual sounds to a modern audience. A refugee for over 30 years, he lives with his wife in the Rocky Mountains of Colorado.

Filled with the philosophy of many cultures and religions, and touching on everything from kindness portrayed in film to scientific support of our limitless capacity for love, kindness, and compassion, this book can truly take the reader on a life-changing journey. Excerpted from Nawang Khechog’s website, nawangkhechog.com
