Sakadawa Nyung Né Retreat

 

June 11-14

led by Wendy Cook

Sakadawa is the Buddhist celebration of the Buddha’s birth, enlightenment and parinirvana. What better way to honor this holiday than to participate in our Nyung Né retreat.

Nyung Né is a beautiful purification practice involving visualization of the Buddha of Compassion (Chenrezig). The three retreat sessions each day incorporate prayers, visualization, reciting mantras, and performing prostrations to help us develop more loving kindness and compassion in our hearts and minds. This retreat includes periods of fasting and strict silence and is a powerful and effective method to both purify an enormous amount of negativity while creating positive conditions for your practice to flourish.

One doesn’t need to have had any initiation prior to this retreat, but it’s best if you have taken Refuge in Buddhism. If you have never attended a Nyung Né before, however, you must attend the evening introduction on June 11.

Please call us at (802) 633-4136 BEFORE registering online to check the prerequisites for this retreat.
