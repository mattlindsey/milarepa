Wheel of Sharp Weapons with Khensur Rinpoche

 

June 20-22

This profound mind-training text was composed by the great yogi Dharmaraksita, a great teacher of Master Atisha. This text effectively strikes at the “heart of the foe,” our self-cherishing thought and grasping at the inherently self-existent “I.” Dharmaraksita transmitted the verses to Master Atisha, who was able to gain great realizations from putting the meaning into practice.

Atisha developed techniques of thought-transformation, such as tonglen, which is the practice of giving and taking. Through understanding this text and embodying these powerful techniques, we have the power to transform our grasping and self-cherishing.

Gyumed Khensur Rinpoche Lobsang Jampa was born in Tibet in 1937 and became a monk at the age of 10. In 1959, he received the Bhikshu vows – the highest level of ordination – from His Eminence, the late Yongzin Ling Rinpoche, the 97th Throne Holder of the Gelugpa Tradition and also senior Tutor to His Holiness the 14th Dalai Lama. Later in 1959, Rinpoche fled the Communist Chinese invasion of Tibet and completed his studies at Sera Mey Monastery in South India. He received a Geshe Lharampa degree as well as the Ngargampa (highest degree in Tantric studies) and was appointed abbot of Gyumed Tantric College in 1996. Rinpoche currently resides at Guhyasamaja Center in Washington, DC, and Do Ngak Kunphen Ling in Redding, CT.
